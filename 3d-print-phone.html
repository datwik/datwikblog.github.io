Can you print your phone?

And I am not talking about a case and things like phone holder. You can download STL files for it and print in your local library today.

I am talking about the phone itself, with all the circuitry, antenna and parts. Well, we can agree on adding battery, sim card and install software ourselves.

According to Ray Kurtzwell and his Singularity theory, we should have been able to do much more than that. Hmm... let's see where we actually are today in 2020.


Source: Time / Sterling Ely

Can you print one today? Well, sort of....

Five years ago, in 2015 Mario the magician printed a phone shell around a $20 phone, because it was cheaper than actually collecting parts together. Check out this makers printed phone:


Source: 3DPrint

Four years ago a factory started printing antenna using Optomec Aerosol Jet technology, which is able to print active electronic components.

Kickstarter has an interesting project of 3d printer called 3DForge that can mix materials, printing electronic circuitry embedded in your device. It was expected to be delivered in Dec 2019, seems very close to completion according to last month update.


Source: Kickstarter

Of course a major hurdle to a 3d printing frenzy would be an array of patents on various physical and software inventions held by various companies. Apple, Samsung and numerous other companies are filing numerous patents as part of their R&D process. Royalties are major revenue stream for them and they wont give it up that easy.

I would really like to come back in 10 years and be able to chose color, texture, screen size, features - and in 2 hours it comes out my phone printed out of the magic machine.

Let's see if the universe delivers!
