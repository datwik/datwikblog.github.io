Build your own mobile computer (Part 2)

Back to the drawing board to clarify requirements: - Need a device that can be power the computing device without a car or an outlet - When driving in the car, it's too shaky to be able to operate keyboard and mouse separately - VS Code should not be closing when switching focus to browser or other program ($300 laptop could do all that, but we pretend it was not invented yet)

For the power source, I went for one of the beasts: Anker PowerCore III Elite 25,600 for $150. Having both USB-C and USB-A outputs allows to power both computer and the monitor, and charge can run for multiple hours. Couple downsides: you can't charge it unless you have a powerful adapter - regular 20W phone adapters won't charge it. And unfortunately you cannot charge and use it at the same time - the moment you insert charging cable it stops providing power. Not exactly laptop experience, but good for now - just need to remember to keep it charged.

Since I heard about Raspberry Pi few years ago I was looking for a cause to try it out. And here you go - got Raspberry Pi 4 Model B 4GB with a case for another $100 or so, connected old Logitech K400r keyboard lying around
