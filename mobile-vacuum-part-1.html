Mobile Vacuum (Part 1)

Mobile Vacuum (Part 1)

Recorded history starts about 6000 years ago in about 4 Millenia BC, with the invention of writing. It took lots of effort and training to be able to record useful amount of information. Some used pottery, metal, stone tablets with cuneiform, tortoise was also was fashionable way of getting message across. It was not very fast, but reliable way to get print a check or knock out a joke if you had a week or so to spare. See, people were a bit more patient then.


Source: Wikimedia

Technologies evolved, we invented papyrus, then paper. Then some little known company figured out that you can store characters as a sequence of tiny magnetic pins on a dish. And it started spinn...


Source: https://www.computerhistory.org

Nowadays we are living fast. Almost every third, if not second person on the planet has access many times the processing power they used to land the man in the moon (whether you believe it or not that it actually happened).

Apart from being convenient and fast, your phone is also rather fascinating contraption. In fact it keeps attention so well, if you ask almost any toddler, they will surely prefer to reach for your colorful glass brick rather than a box of chocolates (try it at your own risk though, your kids might be smarter and try to combine both in one little palm).

There are also few bigger folks very interested at doing anything they can so you keep using the device as long as possible. Sure there are things like Screen Time, but who doesn't disable it first time it tries to gently remind you that you used your phone for 6 hours straight already? You, the proud phone owner are the center of attention. The glorious human being that generates gigabytes of data just by browsing Facebook or playing Pokemon Go.


Now we are not going to dive into reasons on how is this information is being used, stay tuned for part two of this story. Surely it only serves noble causes and handled exactly as described in the Terms of Service (Who clicked the Terms of Service link before hitting Accept? give them Person of the Year award!).

Let's think it this way, if your phone was an octopus, there apps on your phone can be the... little vacuums. (I'm sure octopus would be rather surprised to get such an upgrade, only after few million years of evolution). Your little vac... err... apps consume anything and everything they can get their tiny digital tubies on.


Source: http://www.toxel.com

Location? Sure! All names in your contact list? Give it over. Pictures, text messages.... how do you hold your phone at any single moment upside or horizontal? Vacuums are always near and grateful to hear every single action the great human performed.
