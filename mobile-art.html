Art with mobile phones

Recently I was reading an article about an artist in Germany that took 99 phones and turned them into a functional art project. By driving them around the city in a buggy he managed to make Google maps think that there was a traffic jam formed on the road.



Source: Bored Panda

This put me on a path of a small research, looking to find out what other similar art projects were recently brought to life. Main subject would be projects where device is playing an active role, rather than being a pixel insert in a static installation.

Remembering few months back, CBC Spark podcast had an episode about an artist who developed a mobile app called Die With Me. Based on their experience of getting lost in a city because of dead batter artist Dries Depoorter and developer David Surprenant developed the app that only accepts people in the chat room were the ones with only 5% charge left on their phone. Having anxiety as part of the experience, chat room sparks unusual conversations with strangers accross the world in the same situation.



Source: Dries Depoorter

Another remarkable project, this time photography, involves ... absence of the phones. In this case photographer Eric Pickersgill explores the unnatural position that we take when holding the phone and how lonely we can be while absorbed in the device, even in the presence of other people nearby.



Source: Eric Pickersgill

Concluding project is taken from larger collection of Golan Levin, where phones are involved in creating live performance. During the 28 minute concert, 200 visitor's phones were carefully orchestrated to be dialed by live performers forming various musical patterns.
