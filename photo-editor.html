Photo editor

Long time ago in a galaxy far far away, before there were Amazons, Googles and smartphones there was ... Sketchpad. One of the first, if not the first programs allowing to edit images on the computer screen. Using a light pen, you could draw a picture on a screen of a computer in 1956. This was also a year when Montreal Canadiens won their 8th Stanley Cup Championship and Elvis Presley released his first Gold Album.


Image source: Wikimedia

Many years later, around 1988 that first Photoshop version became commercially available. Since then it became so popular, the name became a verb. Almost anyone who ever seen a digital image on Facebook or Flickr heard the term "photoshopped image". Being the flagship product from it is commercially available from Adobe.


Image source: http://www.guidebookgallery.org

On the journey of finding free alternatives for pretty much every software you can use, today we are looking at GNU Image Manipulation Program (or GIMP for short). Available since 1996, when Space Shuttle Columbia takes up Canadian Robert Thirsk, and Saskatchewan gets the first taste of ADSL high speed internet to be able to download and try GIMP in action.

Unless you are a professional photo editor, chances are that GIMP has more features than you will ever need from a photo editor. Not mentioning simple actions like cropping, rotating and contrast adjustment that you can do directly on your phone, GIMP can help you perform much more advanced digital retouching, artwork creation, compositions ... list goes on.


Image source: https://www.gimp.org

GIMP is backed by an active community of developers and user, with numerous tutorials and videos to help you master all of it's capabilities. On top of that it supports plugins for more advanced editing tasks like adding effects, removing object, textures and RAW editing.
